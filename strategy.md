# Lokam AI — White-Hat Authority Building Strategy

**Goal:** Build Lokam into a recognized entity in the automotive dealership AI category — trusted by Google's knowledge graph, cited by AI answer engines, and credible to dealer principals evaluating software.

**Audience for authority:** Dealer principals, GMs, BDC directors, fixed ops managers, and the algorithm that answers when they ask ChatGPT or Google "best AI for dealership follow-up."

---

## The Core Problem This Strategy Solves

Lokam currently has strong on-page SEO but weak off-page entity signals. That means:
- AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) don't consistently cite Lokam when answering "best AI for CSI follow-up"
- Google's knowledge graph doesn't yet treat Lokam as a distinct, authoritative entity in the automotive AI category
- Dealers searching for reviews or validation find almost nothing outside Lokam's own site

This strategy addresses all three.

## Phase 4 — Community Presence (Month 3-6)
*Build name recognition where dealers actually talk to each other.*

### 4.1 Reddit Strategy

**r/askcarsales** (~59,000 subscribers) is the primary target. Heavily used by sales staff, BDC reps, and some managers. Strict no-spam culture — any promotional post will be removed and hurt credibility.

**The only approach that works:**

1. Create a personal Reddit account for Muhammed Saleeq (not a company account)
2. Build karma by genuinely answering questions about car buying, follow-up, BDC process, customer complaints — topics where Lokam's domain knowledge is real
3. Over time (6-12 weeks of consistent presence), the username becomes trusted
4. When someone posts "what tools does your BDC use?" or "why doesn't my dealer ever call back?" — those are the moments to mention Lokam honestly, in context, as a practitioner
5. Never post unsolicited pitches. Never use a branded account.

**r/Justrolledintotheshop** — service advisor community, less sales-focused. Good for service retention angles.

**r/sales** — general sales community, some BDC/automotive crossover. Higher tolerance for tools discussion.

**r/entrepreneur** and **r/startups** — founder-story posts work here. "We built AI that calls car dealership customers so the BDC doesn't have to" as a Show HN / r/startups post can generate inbound links.

### 4.2 DealerRefresh Forum

DealerRefresh (dealerrefresh.com) is the most respected professional forum for dealership internet/BDC professionals. Unlike Reddit, it explicitly welcomes vendor participation as long as value comes first.

**Strategy:**
1. Saleeq creates an account with full identity (name, company, role)
2. Answer questions about BDC contact rates, CSI processes, DMS integrations — topics where Lokam has direct expertise
3. No direct pitching; vendors who add value over time get invited to contribute

This is the highest-quality professional community available to Lokam. One respected thread in DealerRefresh is worth 50 generic directory listings.

### 4.3 DealersEdge BDC Manager Forum

forums.dealersedge.com — dedicated BDC operations subforum. Smaller than DealerRefresh but more targeted. Same approach: expertise first, company affiliation disclosed, no pitching.

## What This Strategy Does NOT Include (and Why)

- **Paid link placements / sponsored posts framed as editorial** — manipulative, violates Google guidelines, and gets reversed
- **Mass directory submissions** — low-quality directories add noise and can dilute entity signals
- **Generic "AI for business" content** — topical dilution; Lokam's authority must be automotive-dealership-specific or it's worthless
- **Cold email outreach at scale for links** — penalized by Google's spam policies, damages brand with recipients
- **AI-generated review requests** — fake reviews on G2/Capterra result in permanent removal and reputation damage
- **PBN / link network participation** — black-hat, catastrophic if detected
